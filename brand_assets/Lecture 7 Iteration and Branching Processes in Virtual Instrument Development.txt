Lecture 7: Iteration and Branching Processes in Virtual Instrument Development
1. Introduction
Modern engineering applications often require programs to make decisions and perform repetitive operations. In LabVIEW, these tasks are achieved through iteration and branching structures. Iteration allows a program to execute a set of operations repeatedly, while branching enables the program to choose different execution paths based on specific conditions.
Together, these structures form the foundation of dynamic and intelligent Virtual Instruments (VIs) capable of responding to changing inputs, user actions, and real-world conditions.
